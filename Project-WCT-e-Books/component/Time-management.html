Time-Management Books

Eat That Frog

Brian Tracy

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Description: Brian Tracy presents a simple and practical approach to time management and productivity, focusing on the concept of tackling your hardest tasks first (eating the frog). It provides 21 actionable strategies to help you overcome procrastination and increase your efficiency.

Published: 2001 • Aothor: Brian Tracy • Nationality: Canadian-American • Language: English

The 4-Hour Workweek

Tim Ferriss

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Description: Tim Ferriss offers a blueprint for designing a lifestyle that frees up time for more important pursuits. The book emphasizes eliminating time-consuming tasks, automating processes, and focusing on the essential few to achieve more in less time.

Published: April, 2007 • Author: Tim Ferriss • Nationality: American • Language: English

Getting Things Done

David Allen

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Description: David Allen’s GTD (Getting Things Done) method is one of the most popular time management systems. The book details a five-step approach for organizing tasks, clearing mental clutter, and maintaining control over projects and life in general.

Published: 2001 • Author: David Allen • Nationality: American • Language: English

Make Time

Jake Knapp, John Zeratsky

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Description: Written by Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky, this book outlines a simple framework to create time for the things that truly matter. The authors share tips for defeating distractions, setting priorities, and designing your day to optimize productivity.

Published: August 5, 2005 • Author: Jake Knapp, John Zeratsky • Nationality: American • Language: English

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Description:Successful time management is the ability to use time efficiently to achieve goals and tasks. It involves planning, prioritizing, and avoiding distractions. People who are good at managing their time are often more productive and less stressed.
